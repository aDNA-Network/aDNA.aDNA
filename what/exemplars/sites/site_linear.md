# site_linear — Linear

> **The sleek-professional anchor** and the 2026 craft bar. The lesson is *fast-through-restraint*: premium feel comes from refusing ornament, not adding it.

## Captured (rubric)

- **Hero:** ~25 words. "The product development system for teams and agents — purpose-built for planning and building products. Designed for the AI era." Pragmatic, not aspirational.
- **Sections (5):** hero + product preview → narrative arc (Intake → Plan → Build → Diffs → Monitor) → changelog + social proof → footer (30+ links in 6 categories).
- **Above-fold focus:** a **real product preview** (live issue-tracking UI) — demonstrates rather than explains.
- **Visual:** dark interface + precise cyan/blue accents; clean sans-serif, generous whitespace; **real screenshots only, no abstract illustration**; user/team avatars (human presence, not stock).
- **Motion:** minimal/restrained — *feels fast because nothing tries too hard*. No decorative transitions.
- **Density:** medium-sparse; each section isolates one concept; ~1:1 whitespace:content.

## Lift for aDNA

- **Craft = refusal of ornament.** A disciplined palette + precise accents + authentic artifacts signals confidence. Directly supports `visual-identity-v2`'s restraint doctrine and the doctrine's motion budget.
- **Demonstrate, don't explain** — show the *real* thing (for aDNA: a real CLAUDE.md/registry snippet/network diagram), not a marketing abstraction.
- **One-concept-per-section** with breathing room — the section-budget discipline.

## Avoid

- Linear's IA is on the `writing-guidelines` **AVOID** list for *fragmentation* (depth scattered across many sub-pages). Lift the *homepage restraint*, not the deep-IA sprawl.
- Pure product-screenshot strategy presumes a polished GUI product; aDNA's "product" is partly a *standard + network*, so our "real artifact" is text/diagram/registry, not app chrome.
