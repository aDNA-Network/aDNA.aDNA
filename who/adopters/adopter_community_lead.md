# Community Lead

> The steward of the agentic-context *democracy* — focused on self-governance, belonging, and mission, not on running workshops. The person who makes "the Facebook, for good" actually feel like a community for good.

## Background

A community steward whose concern is the **self-governance** of the network — how decisions get made, how members belong, how the mission stays true to the public-good ethos. Distinct from the [[adopter_community_organizer|Community Organizer]] (who is adoption- and workshop-focused): the Community Lead tends the *democracy* — proposals, norms, contribution governance, and the felt sense that this is a movement people want to join *because* it's for the good of the world.

## Goals

- See clear, legible self-governance (how proposals/decisions/norms work)
- Make belonging real — members feel part of a mission, not users of a tool
- Keep the public-good ethos felt across the community surfaces (subtle, not preached)
- Connect mission-aligned subnetworks into one welcoming, values-coherent whole

## Pain Points

- "Community" that's just a Discord link and a logo wall (the HF anti-pattern, shallow)
- Governance that's invisible or opaque — no scent of how the democracy actually works
- Mission language that tips into hype/cringe and loses credibility
- Subnetworks that feel siloed rather than part of a shared movement

## How They Use aDNA

The community/labs/org surface (Track C) + the public-good commons + social layer (Track G):

- **Governance-visible community** — proposals, norms, contribution paths shown, not hidden (the ethereum.org "movement + credible governance" lesson)
- **Public-good showcase** — mission-aligned subnetworks (WGA · Context Commons · WilhelmAI · Rare Archive) presented as a shared movement
- **Join the network** — the invitation to "band together... accelerated by agentic context literacy... for positive impact"

## Ranker emphasis

Primary **relevance** (does this feel like *my* movement?) + secondary **delight** (is belonging warm and real?). Co-owns **Participation Scent** (can I tell how to join/contribute in 60s?).

## Self-reference

The vault's own `who/community/` + `who/governance/` demonstrate self-governance in miniature; the Community Lead surface scales that to the network — the democracy governing itself the way the III loop governs the site's own quality.
