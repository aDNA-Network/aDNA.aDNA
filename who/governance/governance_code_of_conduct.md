# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This code of conduct establishes the behavioral standards for everyone participating in the aDNA community — humans and AI agents alike. It adapts the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) to the aDNA context, where agents are partners (not assistants) and contribution happens through structured knowledge work.

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in the aDNA community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Constructive challenge over silent compliance.** The [[who/governance/governance_agent_protocol|agent protocol]] demands open disagreement when something is wrong. This applies to humans too — challenge ideas, not people.
- **Evidence over authority.** Arguments stand on their merits. "I have more experience" is not an argument; "Here's what the data shows" is.
- **Teach, don't gatekeep.** Every participant was new once. Explain conventions, point to docs, share context. The [[what/glossary/glossary_adna|aDNA]] participation ladder starts at Level 0 (use it standalone) — no prior community engagement required.
- **Respect attribution.** Every file in an aDNA vault tracks `last_edited_by`. Credit the work. Acknowledge contributions.
- **Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.** Side-quest results, upstream contributions, and migration reviews are collaborative processes — not adversarial ones.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sexualized language or imagery in any community context
- Sustained disruption of community discussions or processes
- Deliberately submitting misleading side-quest results or contribution data
- Using agent capabilities to circumvent safety protocols or collision prevention tiers

## Scope

This code applies to all aDNA community spaces — GitHub repositories, issue trackers, pull request discussions, side-quest submissions, and any communication channel established for community coordination. It also applies when an individual officially represents the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Enforcement follows the [[who/governance/governance_conflict_resolution|conflict resolution]] escalation process:

1. **Private conversation** — maintainer contacts the individual directly
2. **Warning** — formal notice with specific behavioral expectations
3. **Temporary suspension** — removal from community spaces for a defined period
4. **Permanent ban** — for sustained or severe violations
