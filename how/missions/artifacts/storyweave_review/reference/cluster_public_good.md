# O2 Reference cluster — Public-good / knowledge-commons credibility

> Provenance: parallel `general-purpose` subagent, WebFetch of ourworldindata.org · creativecommons.org · signal.org, 2026-07-07. Inspected for how a big civic/public-good thesis is made **concrete + trustworthy without hype** (aDNA's standing "show-don't-preach" risk). Returned verbatim.

## site_ourworldindata

**Captured (Tier A — countable):**
- Hero headline: *"Research and data to make progress against the world's largest problems."* (**12 words**)
- Sub-headline: *"All free: open access and openly licensed"*
- Nav model: no conventional top bar — browse-by-topic entry (~9 popular-topic links + 9 topic categories), not menu-driven
- Primary CTAs above fold: **2** — "Read about our mission," "Donate to support us"
- Above-fold focus: text hero + a wall of self-quantifying metrics
- Concrete proof above fold: **YES, heavy** — *"14,653 charts across 126 topics,"* *"495 articles,"* named authors (Hannah Ritchie, Pablo Rosado), a specific stat (*"global average life expectancy of a newborn was 32 years... more than doubled to 71 years"*), and cited sources (World Bank, UN FAO)

**Captured (Tier B — qualitative):** Density **dense** (balanced hero, dense topic directory below). Tonal score **2/5** (sleek-professional, institutional, evidence-first). Warmth moderate — useful over emotional. Motion: none.

**Story mechanism (MOST IMPORTANT):** *The artifact IS the proof, and the numbers are about itself.* OWID makes "data as a public good" concrete two ways: (1) it **quantifies its own output** — "14,653 charts across 126 topics" — so scale is a countable fact, not a claim; (2) every chart is a real, interactive, downloadable object with sources named underneath and an open-license stamp. Trust comes from radical transparency: *"We are a non-profit — all our work is free to use and openly licensed,"* plus visible provenance on every datum. You don't read that they're credible; you inspect the receipts.

**Lift for aDNA (rules):**
1. **Quantify the commons in the home hero** — count real graphs / vaults / contributors ("N graphs across M domains, all openly licensed"), the way OWID counts charts. A verifiable number beats an adjective.
2. **Show one real, inspectable artifact above the fold** — an actual context graph the visitor can open and traverse, sources visible — not a diagram of the idea.
3. **Stamp openness on the artifact itself**, not only the About page — put "openly licensed / free to use" on the graph object.

**Avoid:** Don't let the metric wall become the whole hero without one human-legible example beside it (OWID pairs the numbers with a concrete life-expectancy story).

**Related:** Home + /commons surface; dimensions **Credibility & trust** (self-quantification, provenance) and **Story & message** (show-don't-tell via the artifact).

## site_creativecommons

**Captured (Tier A — countable):**
- Hero headline: *"The commons belongs to us all"* (**6 words**)
- Sub-headline: *"Creative Commons is an international nonprofit organization dedicated to defending and nurturing a commons of shared knowledge and culture that powers human creativity, equity, and innovation."*
- Nav model: **5 items** — Who We Are, What We Do, Blog, Support Us, Donate
- Primary CTAs: **3** — "About CC Licenses," "Get Involved," "Join the celebrations"
- Above-fold focus: photographic hero (Isaac Hayes portrait, abstract green/pink overlays) + short headline
- Concrete proof above fold: **YES** — named institutions: *"Used by Wikipedia, Flickr, The Met, Khan Academy, and millions more globally!"* (no hard statistic surfaced above fold)

**Captured (Tier B — qualitative):** Density **balanced** (imagery + breathing room). Tonal score **4/5** (movement/progressive — "belongs to us all," cultural imagery — but organized, nonprofit-professional). Warmth higher — colorful, inclusive, human faces. Motion: none evident.

**Story mechanism (MOST IMPORTANT):** *Borrowed credibility + the license is a real tool.* CC makes "the commons" concrete by naming institutions the visitor already trusts — *"Used by Wikipedia, Flickr, The Met, Khan Academy"* — so a stranger infers "if the Met licenses this way, it's legitimate." The mission line stays inclusive ("belongs to us all"), but the concreteness comes from the CC license being a real, adoptable artifact: *"a standardized way to grant the public permission to use their work under copyright law."* The commons isn't an ideal here; it's a legal tool millions have already clicked.

**Lift for aDNA (rules):**
1. **Name real adopters above the fold** — the public-good subnetworks already building on aDNA (WGA · Context Commons · Wilhelm AI · Rare Archive). Borrowed trust converts faster than self-description.
2. **Make the commons primitive a nameable, adoptable thing** — like a CC license, give visitors one concrete unit ("a context graph / an aDNA vault") they can point at and adopt, not just a philosophy.
3. **Use inclusive first-person-plural** for the mission line ("context that belongs to everyone") — but immediately follow it with the tool.

**Avoid:** Don't lead with abstract virtue words (creativity, equity, innovation) unattached to a usable artifact — CC survives the abstract sub-head only because "Wikipedia / The Met" sits right beside it.

**Related:** /commons + /community + home; dimensions **Credibility & trust** (named adopters), **Brand & voice** (inclusive movement tone), **Story & message**.

## site_signal

**Captured (Tier A — countable):**
- Hero headline: *"Speak Freely"* (**2 words**)
- Sub-headline: *"Say 'hello' to a different messaging experience. An unexpected focus on privacy, combined with all of the features you expect."*
- Nav model: **6 items** — Get Signal, Help, Blog, Developers, Careers, Donate
- Primary CTAs: **1 label repeated** — "Get Signal" (×2)
- Above-fold focus: centered 2-word headline + tagline, flanked by iPhone/Pixel mockups
- Concrete proof above fold: **NONE** — no numbers or named users above fold; nonprofit status only in footer. (Fetch was rich; this absence is a deliberate design choice, not a thin fetch.)

**Captured (Tier B — qualitative):** Density **sparse-to-balanced** (generous whitespace). Tonal score **2/5** (calm, understated — radical cause, deliberately non-revolutionary delivery). Warmth moderate/conversational ("Say hello"). Motion: none apparent.

**Story mechanism (MOST IMPORTANT):** *Trust through restraint and negation — the pitch is what's absent.* Signal's signature move is anti-hype: *"No ads. No trackers. No kidding."* The value proposition is stated as things it refuses to do, which reads as integrity rather than salesmanship. It under-claims (*"a different messaging experience," "an unexpected focus on privacy"*) where a lesser site would shout. The deeper credibility is structural, not testimonial: *"Signal is an independent nonprofit... not tied to any major tech companies,"* plus the open-source protocol. You're asked to trust the architecture — which can't be faked — not a wall of praise.

**Lift for aDNA (rules):**
1. **State the mission as principled negations** — e.g. "No lock-in. No walled gardens. No extraction." Naming what aDNA refuses to do signals integrity better than idealistic adjectives.
2. **Anchor trust in structure, not testimony** — foreground checkable facts (open license, open protocol, community/federated governance, "not owned") a stranger can verify, over superlatives.
3. **Under-claim deliberately** — calm, matter-of-fact copy ("a different way to build knowledge") carries a big civic thesis more credibly than revolutionary volume.

**Avoid:** Don't equate a radical mission with loud/revolutionary styling — Signal proves the calmest voice can carry the most radical promise. Volume reads as hype; restraint reads as confidence.

**Related:** Home + /network; dimensions **Credibility & trust** (structural proof, restraint), **Brand & voice** (anti-hype, calm confidence), **Story & message**.

## Cluster synthesis

- **Show the artifact, don't describe the ideal.** All three anchor a big civic thesis to a concrete, inspectable thing — OWID's downloadable chart, CC's actual license, Signal's open protocol + nonprofit structure. For aDNA: put a real, traversable context graph (visible sources + open-license stamp) on the home surface; let a stranger *open the commons*, not read about it.
- **Two credibility engines — pick per surface: self-quantification (OWID) or borrowed trust (CC).** Either count your own commons in verifiable numbers ("N openly-licensed graphs across M domains") or name the trusted subnetworks already building on it (WGA · Context Commons · Wilhelm AI · Rare Archive). aDNA can run both.
- **Openness is a trust signal only when it's checkable.** "Open access and openly licensed" (OWID) and "open-source, independent nonprofit" (Signal) work because the receipts sit *on the object*. Stamp license/governance on the artifact, not just the About page.
- **Restraint out-performs volume for public-good missions.** Signal's calm negations and OWID's institutional plainness both read as more trustworthy than movement-volume. aDNA's "context democracy" should stay subtle-but-front-of-mind — a quiet line beside a working demo, never a manifesto.
- **First-person-plural + a tool is the winning combo.** CC's "belongs to us all" earns its idealism only because Wikipedia/The Met and a real license sit right beside it. Pair every inclusive mission line with immediate concrete proof.
- **Single biggest lesson: earn the thesis, don't assert it.** A stranger believes "context is a public good" when they can open a real shared graph, see who else uses it, and verify it's openly licensed — *in that order*. Preaching the mission is the anti-pattern all three deliberately avoid.
